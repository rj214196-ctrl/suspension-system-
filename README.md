# Vehicle Suspension System

## Overview
This project focuses on the study and conceptual design of a **vehicle suspension system** used to improve ride comfort, vehicle stability, and road handling. The suspension system absorbs shocks from uneven road surfaces and maintains proper contact between the tires and the road.

The project demonstrates the working principle, components, and importance of suspension systems in automobiles.

---

## Objectives
- To understand the working principle of a vehicle suspension system.
- To study the major components used in suspension assemblies.
- To analyze how suspension systems improve **ride comfort and vehicle stability**.
- To explore the relationship between **spring stiffness, damping, and vehicle performance**.

---

## Suspension System Components
The main components studied in this project include:

- **Spring**
  - Stores energy when compressed and absorbs road shocks.
- **Shock Absorber (Damper)**
  - Controls oscillations produced by the spring.
- **Control Arms**
  - Connect the wheel assembly to the vehicle chassis.
- **Wheel Hub**
  - Supports the wheel and allows rotation.
- **Suspension Linkages**
  - Maintain wheel alignment and stability.

---

## Working Principle
The suspension system works by absorbing road irregularities through **springs and dampers**.

1. When the vehicle moves over a bump, the **spring compresses** and absorbs energy.
2. The **shock absorber (damper)** controls the spring motion and prevents excessive bouncing.
3. This reduces vibrations transmitted to the vehicle body.
4. The system maintains **tire contact with the road**, improving handling and stability.
